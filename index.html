Notable Projects


SunCam (Graduate Research)

The SunCam was developed as a low cost sun tracking sky imager for future use in solar forecasting. Current sky image based forecasts are mostly based on instrumentation costing an upwards of $30,000 and are also ground-stationary, while this current model can be built for under $300 and is dynamic. The SunCam tracks the movement of the sun, increasing the effective area around the sun used in the stochastic models. Tracking the movement of the sun also allows for further telemetry of DNI (Direct Normal Irradiance), another costly instrument in the field.
The SunCam tracking system was developed on a Linux based single-board computer, the Beaglebone Black. The stand alone system can be connected to a network, and remotely calibrated and controlled using an in house UI that I had developed to accompany the system. To account for any power outages or mechanical issues, accelerometer and magnetometer sensors detect any deviations from the systems intended path and report back to the user.


Portable Desalination Unit
(Environmental Engineering Design Project)

The Portable Desalination Unit was designed as a proof of concept of using thermal energy to remove pure water from oceanic water. With teammembers Jacob Sanchez, Leyden Recinos, Kevin Park, and Mio Unno, we developed a prototype of a portable desalination unit based on the technology of a solar collector and an understanding of fluid dynamics. The portable unit is free from any grid energy requirements can be installed wherever solar energy is present.
